### 5.1 Collaborative Principled Negotiation and Its Four Components
### 5.1 合作原则谈判法及其四大组成部分

**[EN]** The increasing acceptance of the win-win concept has brought forth the development of new negotiation theories. A representative one is **Collaborative Principled Negotiation (CPN)**, also commonly known as Harvard Principled Negotiation, established by **Roger Fisher** and **William Ury**, professors from Harvard University. Their theoretical system is especially reflected in the famous book *Getting to Yes: Negotiating Agreement Without Giving In*, which is widely regarded as the "Bible" of negotiations.

**[中文]** 随着双赢理念的广泛认可，新型谈判理论不断涌现。其中最具代表性的是**合作原则谈判法（CPN）**，又称"哈佛原则谈判法"，由哈佛大学教授**罗杰·费希尔**与**威廉·尤里**创立。其理论集中体现于著作《*Getting to Yes*》（谈判力：无需让步的协议达成之道），被誉为谈判领域的"圣经"。

---

**[EN - Core Definition]** The core of CPN is to reach a solution beneficial to both parties by stressing interests and values, but **not** by haggling. It decides issues on their merits rather than through a positional bargaining process. It suggests you look for mutual gains whenever possible; when interests conflict, insist that the result be based on **fair standards independent of the will of either side**. CPN is *hard on the merits, soft on the people*. It employs no tricks and no posturing.

**[中文 - 核心定义]** 合作原则谈判法的核心在于：通过强调各方的**利益与价值**（而非讨价还价），达成对双方均有利的方案。它根据问题的是非曲直做出决定，而非在立场上角力。它建议谈判者尽量寻求互利；当利益冲突时，坚持结果必须基于**独立于任何一方意志的公平标准**。CPN 的精髓是：**对问题强硬，对人温和**，不耍手段，不摆姿态。

---

**[EN - Four Components]** Collaborative principled negotiation consists of **four basic components**:
1. **People**: Separate the people from the problem.
2. **Interests**: Focus on interests, not positions.
3. **Gaining**: Invent options for mutual gain.
4. **Criteria**: Introduce objective criteria.

The four components are interrelated and should be applied to the **whole course** of the negotiations.

**[中文 - 四大组成部分]** 合作原则谈判法由**四大基本要素**构成：
1. **人（People）**：把人与问题分开
2. **利益（Interests）**：着眼于利益，而非立场
3. **方案（Gaining）**：创造双赢方案
4. **标准（Criteria）**：引入客观评判标准

四个要素相互关联，应贯穿**谈判全程**加以运用。

---

### 5.2 Separate the People from the Problem（对事不对人）

**[EN - Overview]** It is generally understood that negotiations go smoothly in a friendly and sincere atmosphere. Unfortunately, more often than not, high tension is built up due to negotiators' **prejudice** against the other party or **poor impression** and **misled interpretation** of the other party's intentions. It is conceivable that negotiations will be directed to personal disputes.

**[中文 - 概述]** 通常认为，谈判应在友好、真诚的氛围中顺利推进。然而现实中，由于谈判者对对方存有**偏见**、形成**不良印象**或对其意图产生**误解**，谈判往往充满紧张对立，甚至演变为人身攻击。

**[EN]** A basic fact about a negotiation is that you are not dealing with abstract representatives of the other side, but are dealing with **human beings**. They have emotions, deeply held values, and different backgrounds and viewpoints — and they are **unpredictable**. This human aspect can be either helpful or disastrous.

**[中文]** 谈判中一个基本事实是：你面对的不是抽象的"对方代表"，而是活生生的**人**——他们有情感、有根深蒂固的价值观、有各自不同的背景和观点，而且**无法预测**。这种人性因素既可以促进谈判，也可能让谈判崩溃。

To find your way through people problems, think in terms of **three basic categories: perception, emotion, and communication**.

处理人际问题，需从**三个基本维度**入手：**认知（Perception）、情绪（Emotion）、沟通（Communication）**。

---

#### 5.2.1 Perception（认知）

**[EN]** Understanding the other side's thinking is not simply a useful activity — their thinking **is the problem**. Conflict lies not in objective reality, but in **people's heads**.

**Put yourself in their shoes.** People tend to pick out and focus on facts that confirm their prior perceptions and to disregard or misinterpret those that call their perceptions into question.

**Do not blame them for your problem.** Blaming is an easy mode to fall into. But even if justified, it is usually counterproductive. Under attack, the other side will become defensive and will resist what you have to say.

**Give the other side a stake in the outcome.** If the other party is not involved in the process, it is hardly likely to approve the product. The feeling of **participation** in the process is perhaps the single most important factor in determining whether a negotiator accepts a proposal. In a sense, the **process is the product**.

**[中文]** 理解对方的思维方式，不仅仅是有益的——他们的思维方式本身**就是问题所在**。冲突并不存在于客观现实中，而存在于**人的头脑中**。

**换位思考（Put yourself in their shoes）**：人们总倾向于关注能印证自身既有认知的信息，而忽略或曲解那些对其认知构成挑战的信息。

**不要因为自己的问题而责备对方**：责备是最容易陷入的模式，但即便有理，也往往适得其反——对方会变得防御，甚至反击。

**让对方参与到结果中来**：若对方没有参与整个谈判过程，就很难认可最终结果。**参与感**是决定谈判者是否接受方案的最重要因素之一。在某种意义上，**过程即产品**。

---

#### 5.2.2 Emotion（情绪）

**[EN]** In a negotiation, particularly in a bitter dispute, feelings may be more important than talk. Emotions on one side will generate emotions on the other. Emotions may quickly bring a negotiation to an impasse or an end.

**Allow the other side to let off steam.** One effective way to deal with anger, frustration, and other negative emotions is to help them release those feelings. The best strategy while the other side lets off steam is to listen quietly without responding to their attacks.

**Do not react to emotional outbursts.** Releasing emotions can prove risky if it leads to an emotional reaction. An unusual and effective technique: adopt the rule that only **one person can get angry at a time** — this makes it legitimate for others not to respond stormily, and has the advantage of helping people control their emotions.

**[中文]** 在谈判中，尤其是激烈的争议中，**情绪**往往比言辞更重要。一方的情绪会引发另一方的情绪连锁反应，从而迅速将谈判推向僵局甚至崩溃。

**允许对方发泄（Let off steam）**：处理愤怒、挫败等负面情绪最有效的方式之一，就是帮助对方释放情绪。最佳策略是安静倾听，不回应攻击。

**不要对情绪爆发做出情绪性反应**：情绪失控本身是有风险的。一个有效技巧是规定**同一时刻只能有一人发怒**，这为他人不做激烈回应提供了"正当理由"，也有助于双方控制情绪。

---

#### 5.2.3 Communication（沟通）

**[EN]** There are three big problems in communication:
① Negotiators may not be talking to each other, or at least not in such a way as to be understood.
② Even if talking directly and clearly, they may not be **listening**.
③ The third problem is **misunderstanding**.

**Listen actively and acknowledge what is being said.** Listening enables you to understand their perceptions, feel their emotions, and hear what they are trying to say. The cheapest concession you can make to the other side is to **let its negotiators know they have been heard**.

**Speak about yourself, not about them.** It is more persuasive to describe a problem in terms of its impact on *you* than in terms of what the negotiator of the other side did or why. "I feel let down" instead of "You are a racist."

**Avoid trying to score points and debating them as opponents.** It is unpersuasive to blame the other party for the problem, to engage in name-calling, or to raise your voice.

**[中文]** 沟通存在三大核心障碍：
① 双方可能根本没在真正交流，或至少没有以能被理解的方式交流；
② 即便在直接、清晰地表达，对方也可能没有在**倾听**；
③ 第三个问题是**误解**。

**积极倾听并认可对方所说的内容**：倾听能让你理解对方的认知、感受其情绪、听懂其真实意图。你能给对方最"廉价"的让步，就是**让对方感到被倾听**。

**谈论自己，而非评价对方**：用陈述某件事对*你*的影响来描述问题，比指责对方的行为或动机更有说服力。例如说"我感到失望"，而非"你是个种族主义者"。

**避免争辩和给对方打分**：指责对方、人身攻击、提高嗓门，这些都是无说服力的行为。

---

**[EN - Summary of 5.2]** In general, to separate people from problems, the crucial point is to understand the other party, control one's own emotion and strengthen communication. We look for other party, control our counterpart afterwards if their opinion is not right; we allow them chances to correct our counterpart if they feel upset; we treat our counterpart as a **cooperator sitting on the same boat** — sinking and floating together, moving toward **mutual success hand in hand**.

**[中文 - 5.2小结]** 总体而言，要做到"对事不对人"，关键在于**理解对方、控制自身情绪、加强沟通**。我们把对方视为同乘一船的**合作伙伴**——同舟共济，携手迈向共同成功。

---

### 5.3 Focus on Interests But Not Positions（着眼于利益，而非立场）

**[EN - Core Concept]** **Position** refers to the preset attitude towards something or a choice of sides. Fisher and Ury distinguish the difference between interest and position through the classic story of **two men quarreling in a library**: one wants the window open, the other wants it closed. They argue about how much to leave it open: a crack, halfway, three quarters. No solution satisfies them both. A librarian enters, asks one why he wants it open ("To get some fresh air"), asks the other why he wants it closed ("To avoid the draft"). She opens wide a window **in the next room**, bringing in fresh air without a draft. This story is typical of many negotiations — since the parties' goal is to agree on a **position**, they naturally tend to reach an **impasse**.

**[中文 - 核心概念]** **立场（Position）** 是指对某件事预先持有的态度或立场选择。费希尔与尤里通过一个经典故事阐明了立场与利益的区别：**图书馆里两位读者争执**——一人要开窗，另一人要关窗，双方就开多大争执不休，互不满意。图书管理员分别询问各自原因：一人要新鲜空气，另一人要避免穿堂风。管理员于是**打开隔壁房间的窗户**，既引入新鲜空气，又避免穿堂风。这个故事揭示了谈判的典型困境：当双方都执着于**立场**时，僵局往往难以避免。

**[EN]** Successful negotiations are the result of mutual giving and taking of **interests** rather than keeping firm on one's own position. The method of focusing on common interests works because: 
① There is always **more than one way** of fulfilling each other's interests;  
② Both sides can always find out **certain common interests**, otherwise they will not sit together discussing and talking.

**[中文]** 成功的谈判源于双方在**利益**层面的相互给予与接纳，而非固守立场。聚焦共同利益之所以有效，是因为：
① 满足双方利益的方式**不止一种**；
② 双方总能找到**某些共同利益**——否则根本不会坐到谈判桌前。

---

#### 5.3.1 Identify Interests（明确利益）

**[EN]**
- **Explore their interests which stood in our way**: A position is likely to be concrete and explicit; the interests underlying it may well be unexpressed, intangible, and perhaps inconsistent. Ask yourself "Why?" — your purpose is to understand the **needs, hopes, fears, or desires** that the position serves.
- **Examine the different interests of different people on their side**: Almost every negotiation has many interests, not just one. A common error is to assume everyone on the other side has the *same* interests.
- **Look at their human needs underlying their positions**: Look particularly for those bedrock concerns which motivate all people — **security, economic well-being, a sense of belonging, recognition, and control over one's life**. Basic human needs are easy to overlook; we tend to think the only interest is money.

**[中文]**
- **探寻阻碍我方的对方利益**：立场往往具体而明确，其背后的利益却可能是隐性的、无形的甚至是矛盾的。要不断追问"为什么"——目的是理解立场背后的**需求、希望、恐惧或愿望**。
- **审视对方不同人的不同利益**：几乎每场谈判都不止一种利益诉求。一个常见错误是假设对方所有人都有相同的利益。
- **透过立场看到背后的人性需求**：特别关注那些驱动所有人的基本需求——**安全感、经济利益、归属感、被认可感、以及对自己生活的掌控感**。基本人性需求很容易被忽视，我们往往误以为对方唯一的利益就是金钱。

---

#### 5.3.2 Talk about Interests（讨论利益）

**[EN]** The purpose of negotiating is to serve your interests. The chance of achieving this purpose increases when you **communicate them**.
- Make your interests come alive. Acknowledge their interests as part of the problem.
- **Be hard on the problem, soft on the people.** It may not be wise to commit yourself to your position, but it is wise to commit yourself to your **interests**. Often the wisest solutions, which produce the maximal gain for you at the minimal cost to the other side, are produced only by **strongly advocating your interests**.

**[中文]** 谈判的目的是服务于自身利益，而这一目标实现的前提是**将利益清晰地传达出去**。
- 让你的利益"活起来"，将对方的利益作为共同问题的一部分加以认可。
- **对问题强硬，对人温和**。固守立场不一定明智，但坚守**利益**才是明智之举。最聪明的解决方案——对你收益最大、对对方代价最小——往往只有通过**强力倡导自身利益**才能实现。

---

### 5.4 Invent Options for Mutual Gain（创造双赢方案）

**[EN - Overview]** The first two components look at the relation between people and problems, and interests and positions — which are conducive for negotiators to establish an objective view. The **third component** of inventing options for mutual gain provides an approach to the fulfillment of the two parties' demands.

**[中文 - 概述]** 前两个要素分别处理"人与问题的关系"以及"利益与立场的关系"，帮助谈判者建立客观视角。**第三个要素**——创造双赢方案——则为满足双方需求提供了具体路径。

**[EN - Why negotiators get trapped]** Many negotiations focus on a single event, and the solution is either winning or losing (e.g., price of a car, size of a commission). The **distributive nature** of interest gaining limits people's scope of thinking. There are **three major barriers** to inventing creative options:

① **The fixed plan (固定馅饼假设)**: Both sides perceive the size of the cake is fixed — "my gain is your loss." This rigid distributive concept retards creative thinking.

② **Seeking only one solution (只寻求单一答案)**: Negotiators rest on their laurels and hope to arrive at the final solution without other nuisances. They are not aware that creative thinking and options are an **indispensable** part of a successful negotiation.

③ **Considering only one's own options (只顾自身利益)**: A successful negotiation is a process of giving and taking — options provided should be a **consolidated body of both sides' interests**.

**[中文 - 为何谈判者容易陷入困境]** 许多谈判聚焦于单一事件，结果非赢即输（如汽车价格、佣金比例）。利益分配的**零和特性**限制了人们的思维空间。创造性方案的生成面临**三大障碍**：

① **固定馅饼假设**：双方都觉得利益总量固定——"你多我就少"。这种僵化的分配思维扼杀了创造性思考。

② **只寻求单一答案**：谈判者急于找到"那个答案"，不愿意付出创造多种选项的努力，没有意识到创意与选项是谈判成功**不可缺少**的组成部分。

③ **只考虑满足自身需求的方案**：成功的谈判是相互给予的过程，方案必须是**综合双方利益**的整体。

---

#### 5.4.1 Invent Creative Options（发明创造性方案）

**[EN]**
**(1) Separate inventing options from evaluating them.**
Since judgment hinders imagination, separate the **creative act** from the **critical one**. Invent first, decide later. Consider arranging a **brainstorming session** — designed to produce as many ideas as possible. The key ground rule is to **postpone all criticism and evaluation** of ideas. With those inhibitions removed, one idea should stimulate another, **like firecrackers setting off one by one**.

**(2) Develop several options before looking for a solution.**
A brainstorming session frees people to think creatively. The key to wise decision-making lies in selecting from a **great number and variety of options**. Sources of options can come from considerations on both general and specific thinking: finding out the problems first, analyzing the problems second, considering what to do next, and finally coming up with specific and feasible suggestions for action.

**[中文]**
**(1) 将创造方案与评价方案分开进行**
评判会束缚想象力，因此要将**创意行为**与**批判行为**分开。先发明，后决定。可以组织**头脑风暴会议**——目标是尽可能多地产生想法。核心规则是**暂停一切批评与评价**。当这些束缚被解除后，一个想法应当激发另一个想法，**如鞭炮一串串炸响**。

**(2) 在寻找解决方案之前先发展多个选项**
头脑风暴让人们自由发散。明智决策的关键在于从**大量多样的选项**中筛选。选项的来源可以从一般性和具体性两个层面获取：先发现问题、分析问题、思考对策，最终提出具体可行的行动建议。

---

#### 5.4.2 Look for Mutual Gain（寻求互利）

**[EN]** A major block to creative problem-solving lies in the assumption of a **fixed pie**: the less for you, the more for me. Actually, there **almost always exists** the possibility of joint gain.

**(1) Identify shared interests.**
Three points about shared interests are worth remembering:
- First, shared interests **lie latent** in every negotiation.
- Second, shared interests are **opportunities, not godsends** — make a shared interest explicit and formulate it as a **shared goal**, making it concrete and future-oriented.
- Third, stressing your shared interests can make the negotiation smoother and more amicable. *Passengers in a lifeboat afloat in the middle of the ocean with limited rations will subordinate their differences over food in pursuit of their shared interests in getting to shore.*

**(2) Look for options that will make the decision easier for them.**
Since success for you depends upon the other side's decision, you should do what you can to make that decision **an easy one** for them. Rather than make things difficult, confront the other side with a choice that is **as painless as possible**.

**[中文]** 创造性解决问题的最大障碍，是**固定馅饼**的假设：你多得，我就少得。然而实际上，**几乎总存在**共同获益的可能。

**(1) 找到共同利益**
关于共同利益，有三点值得牢记：
- 第一，共同利益**潜伏在每一场谈判之中**；
- 第二，共同利益是**机遇，不是天赐之物**——要将共同利益明确化，把它表述为**共同目标**，使其具体且面向未来；
- 第三，强调共同利益能让谈判更顺畅、更友好。*就像在大洋中漂流的救生艇上，乘客们会因为上岸这一共同利益而搁置食物分配上的分歧。*

**(2) 寻找能让对方更容易做决定的选项**
你的成功有赖于对方的决定，因此你应该尽一切努力让对方的决定**变得容易**。与其制造障碍，不如让对方面对一个**尽可能无痛苦的选择**。

### 5.5 Introduce Objective Criteria（引入客观评判标准）

**[EN - Overview]** The first three components advocate considering both parties' interests and designing constructive patterns. However, conflicts and disputes will not disappear no matter how creative the two sides are. When the two sides cannot decide which option is reasonable and rational, looking for an **objective criterion** will be a way out.

**[中文 - 概述]** 前三个要素都在倡导兼顾双方利益、设计建设性方案。然而，无论双方多么富有创意，冲突与争议都不会消失。当双方无法判断哪个方案合理、理性时，寻找**客观评判标准**便成为出路。

**[EN - Classic Case: India vs. US at an Ocean Law Conference]** 
India and US were arguing about whether ocean mineral exploring companies should pay an initial fee. India (representing developing countries) insisted on $60 million per site; US rejected firmly, suggesting no initial fee. They could not reach an agreement. Later, a representative found a **model for the economics of deep-seabed mining by MIT** which could calculate all payment proposals and their impacts on the economic return. The model was accepted as an **objective criterion** by both sides — it showed India's proposal of initial fee would actually interrupt a company's normal operation (since they'd have to pay the fee five years before generating any revenue). The Indian representative agreed to reconsider; the MIT model also showed US that an initial fee of reasonable sum was possible economically. **Result: US gave up its position.**

**[中文 - 经典案例：国际海洋法会议上的印美谈判]**
在一次国际海洋法会议上，印度与美国就海洋矿产开采公司是否应缴纳初始费用产生争议。印度（代表发展中国家）坚持每个开采点收取6000万美元；美国坚决拒绝，主张免收初始费用。谈判陷入僵局。后来，一位代表发现了**麻省理工学院（MIT）建立的深海采矿经济模型**，可以计算各种付费方案及其对经济回报的影响。该模型被双方接受为**客观评判标准**——它显示印度提议的初始费用实际上会打断公司的正常运营。印度代表同意重新考虑；MIT模型同样告知美方，合理数额的初始费用在经济上是可行的。**结果：美方放弃了原来的立场。**

#### 5.5.1 Developing Objective Criteria（建立客观标准）

**[EN] (1) Look for fair standards.**
An objective criterion should be fair, effective, and rational. The following points will be considered when telling if a criterion is objective or not:
- ① An objective criterion should be **independent of the wills of all parties** and thus be free from sentimental influence of any one.
- ② An objective criterion should be **legitimate and practical**. In a boundary dispute, for example, you may find it easier to agree on a physically outstanding feature such as a river than on a line three yards to the east of the riverbank.
- ③ An objective criterion should be **at least theoretically accepted by both sides**, as is in the case of the MIT model.

Different issues have different objective criteria. For price negotiations, criteria include cost, market situation, depreciation, price competition and other necessary factors. In other negotiations, experts' opinions, international conventions, norms and legal documents will all serve as objective criteria.

**[中文] (1) 寻找公平标准**
客观标准应当公平、有效、理性。判断一个标准是否客观，可参考以下三点：
- ① 客观标准应**独立于所有各方的意志之外**，不受任何一方情感因素的影响；
- ② 客观标准应具有**正当性且切实可行**。例如在边界争端中，以一条河流为分界线，比以"河岸以东三码"更容易被双方接受；
- ③ 客观标准应**至少在理论上被双方所接受**，MIT模型正是如此。

不同议题有不同的客观标准。价格谈判中，成本、市场行情、折旧、价格竞争等均可作为标准；其他谈判中，专家意见、国际惯例、行业规范和法律文件也都可以作为客观标准。

**[EN] (2) Look for fair procedures.**
To produce an outcome independent of wills, you can use either fair standards for the substantive questions or **fair procedures** for resolving the conflicting interests. One example of fair procedure is when one party cuts a cake, **ask the other party to choose first**. Other procedures which may be called fair can be "doing it in turns" or "drawing lots." Drawing lots, flipping a coin and other forms of chance have an inherent fairness — the results may be unequal, but each side has an **equal opportunity**.

**[中文] (2) 寻求公平程序**
为了产生独立于双方意志的结果，可以使用公平标准，也可以使用**公平程序**来解决利益冲突。一个公平程序的例子是：一方切蛋糕，**另一方先挑选**。其他公平程序包括"轮流"或"抽签"。抽签、掷硬币等方式本身具有内在公平性——结果可能不均等，但每一方都有**平等的机会**。

---

#### 5.5.2 Standards for Successful Negotiations（成功谈判的三项标准）

**[EN]** In Fisher and Ury's view, the **three standards** below can be applied to judging success or failure of a negotiation approach:
1. If an agreement is possibly reached, it should **satisfy the legitimate interest of both parties** to the maximum and resolve their conflicts, meanwhile **protecting public interests**.
2. The agreement should be **highly efficient**.
3. The agreement will **improve, or at least not hurt, the relationship** of the two parties.

**[中文]** 费希尔与尤里认为，可以用以下**三项标准**来判断一种谈判方式的成败：
1. 如果能达成协议，它应该**最大限度地满足双方的正当利益**、解决冲突，同时**维护公众利益**；
2. 协议应当**高效**；
3. 协议应当**改善，或至少不损害**双方的关系。

---

**[EN - Final Summary]** CPN provides us with a way to reach a wise agreement for tough negotiations. It has proved to be suitable to almost all situations — from international negotiations to domestic and private negotiations, from simple events to complex situations, from routine talks to urgent meetings. CPN can be used by diplomats, lawyers, business executives, and even couples deciding how to divide their property.

Unlike almost all other strategies, if the other side learns this one, it does not become more difficult to use; **it becomes easier**. The success of CPN does not rely on playing tricks or negotiators' resourcefulness, but on **fairness, objectiveness, and mutual understanding**.

**[中文 - 全章总结]** 合作原则谈判法为艰难谈判提供了达成明智协议的路径。它适用于几乎所有情境——从国际谈判到国内和私人谈判，从简单事务到复杂情境，从日常会谈到紧急会议。外交官、律师、商界人士乃至处理离婚财产分割的夫妻都可以运用CPN。

与几乎所有其他策略不同，如果对方也学会了这种方法，它不会变得更难使用，反而会**更加容易**。CPN的成功不依赖于耍手段或谈判者的机智，而依赖于**公平、客观与相互理解**。
